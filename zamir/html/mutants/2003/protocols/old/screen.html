Phenotyping platforms

 Phenotyping is the rate-limiting step in such large-scale mutagenesis studies.
Our approach is to develop a stepwise protocol for extensive phenotypic analysis
aimed at capturing data and images describing the entire life cycle of the plants.
The first phenotypic screen is conducted in M2 generation and is based on visual
characterization of the plants at all developmental stages from the nursery until
fruit maturation in the field. We use the TGC phenotypic classes
It should be noted that this screen in family structure allows to isolate not only
variation of major effects but also minor 'QTL like' mutations.

Verification of a monogenic mutation, as described in M2, and a detailed quantitative
phenotyping will be conducted in M3 generation.
The detailed phenotyping will be conducted in the field for analyzing morphological
variation as well as yield-associated traits.

Here are the major categories that each mutant was assigned into:
  • Anthocyanin modifications: intensification, reduction, elimination
  • Chlorophyll deficiency
  • Virescent: chlorophyll deficiency localized at growing point
  • Variegation, flecking or striping
  • Leaf form and size
  • Plant habit and size
  • Flower form and color
  • Inflorescence
  • Sterility: any condition leading to partial or complete unfruitfulness
  • Fruit form and surface texture
  • Fruit color and flavor, ripening modification
  • Disease and stress response
  • Foliage color
  • Vascular modification
